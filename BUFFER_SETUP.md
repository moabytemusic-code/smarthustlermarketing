You asked if you should use Buffer's RSS feed option instead of Zapier.
**YES.** Since you are on the "Smart Hustler" plan (minimizing costs/complexity), Buffer's native tool is superior because:
1.  **It's Free:** Included in the free plan (up to 3 feeds).
2.  **No Middleman:** You don't need Zapier to "talk" to Buffer.
3.  **Visual:** You can see the posts in your queue before they go live.

## üõ† Step 1: Connect Your Feed in Buffer

1.  Log in to [Buffer.com](https://buffer.com).
2.  Click on **Publishing** (top left tab).
3.  On the left sidebar, look for your Channel (e.g., your Twitter or LinkedIn profile).
    *   *Note:* You must do this for *each* channel you want to auto-post to.
4.  Click on the **Settings** tab (gear icon) for that channel.
    *   *If you don't see "Feeds" in Settings, look for a "Content" or "Feeds" tab directly in the main dashboard. Buffer moves this UI often.*
5.  Enter your RSS Feed URL:
    ```
    https://smarthustlermarketing.com/rss.xml
    ```
6.  Click **Add Feed**.

---

## ‚öôÔ∏è Step 2: Configure Auto-Posting

Once the feed is added, Buffer gives you two choices. **This is the critical part.**

### Option A: "Add to Queue" (Fully Automated)
*   **What it does:** When you publish a blog post, Buffer automatically creates a social post and puts it in the *next available slot* in your queue.
*   **Pros:** Set and forget.
*   **Cons:** You rely on Buffer's default text (usually just the Title + Link).
*   **How to set:** Look for a checkbox or toggle that says **"Add to Queue automatically"** or **"Enable Link Attachment"**.

### Option B: "Review First" (Recommended for Quality)
*   **What it does:** It pulls the blog post into a "Feeds" list inside Buffer. You have to click "Add to Queue" manually.
*   **Pros:** You can write a custom hook (e.g., "Just dropped a new guide...") instead of just "New Post: Title".
*   **Cons:** Not 100% automated.

## üìù Recommended Workflow for You

Since you want "Set & Forget" but with high quality:

1.  **Use Buffer's Feed for Twitter/X:**
    *   Set it to **Auto-Add to Queue**.
    *   Why? Twitter is high volume; a simple Title + Link is acceptable.

2.  **Use Buffer's Feed for LinkedIn:**
    *   Set it to **Manual Review**.
    *   Why? LinkedIn requires a "human" touch. When you see the post in Buffer, click "Add to Queue" but add a unique 2-sentence insight first.
